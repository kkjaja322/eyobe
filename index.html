🧠 Critical Thinking — EtCO₂ Physiology
Why does EtCO₂ drop during hyperventilation?
Mechanism: ↑ Minute ventilation (↑RR or ↑Vt) → CO₂ washout exceeds production → ↓PaCO₂ → ↓EtCO₂.

Clinical significance: Deliberate hyperventilation ↓ICP via cerebral vasoconstriction (useful in acute herniation), but sustained hyperventilation → respiratory alkalosis → cerebral ischaemia. PaCO₂ target in ICP management: 35–40 mmHg (permissive) or 30–35 mmHg (therapeutic, short-term).

Capnography waveform cue: A plateau that never fully elevates (shark-fin pattern) suggests bronchospasm or incomplete exhalation — not hyperventilation.

Regional anesthesia techniques offer excellent surgical anesthesia and postoperative analgesia while minimizing opioid requirements and systemic side effects.

💉 Spinal Anesthesia (Subarachnoid Block)
Needle
25G–27G Whitacre/Quincke
Space
L3–L4 or L4–L5
Drug
0.5% Heavy Bupivacaine
Volume
2–3 mL
Onset
5–10 minutes
Duration
2–4 hours
Level Target
T4–T10 (surgery dependent)
Adjuvants
Fentanyl 10–25 mcg, Morphine 100–200 mcg
1Position patient — sitting or lateral decubitus, maximal lumbar flexion ("fetal position")
2Identify L3–L4 interspace (at level of iliac crests — Tuffier's line)
3Strict aseptic technique — chlorhexidine prep, sterile drape, sterile gloves
4Local infiltration with 1% lidocaine at insertion point
5Advance spinal needle through skin → subcutaneous → supraspinous ligament → interspinous ligament → ligamentum flavum → epidural space → dura → subarachnoid space
6Confirm CSF freely flowing; inject local anesthetic ± adjuvant
7Position patient appropriately for desired block level; assess sensory level with ice/pinprick
8Monitor BP every 2 min for 15 min — hypotension common (treat with IV fluids/vasopressors)
⚠ Contraindications: Patient refusal, coagulopathy (INR >1.5, platelets <80,000), infection at site, raised ICP, uncorrected hypovolaemia, severe AS/MS (relative)

🤔 Clinical Critical Thinking Prompt

Ponder: How does a patient's kidney or liver function alter these drug interaction parameters? Consider that reduced renal clearance prolongs drug action and raises plasma concentrations, while hepatic impairment affects first-pass metabolism, protein binding (↓albumin → ↑free drug fraction), and cytochrome P450 activity — all of which can intensify or prolong interactions not flagged in healthy-population databases.

CrCl < 30 mL/min? Child-Pugh Class? Protein binding ↓? CYP450 substrate? Narrow therapeutic index?

Key Volatile Concepts

MAC
Minimum Alveolar Concentration — the % at which 50% of patients do not respond to a standard surgical incision
Blood:Gas Coefficient
Lower = faster onset/offset (desflurane fastest). Reflects solubility in blood — less soluble = faster equilibration
Oil:Gas Coefficient
Reflects lipid solubility — predicts anesthetic potency (Meyer-Overton rule). Higher = more potent
MH Triggers
All halogenated volatiles + succinylcholine trigger MH in susceptible individuals. Use TIVA + non-depolarizing NMBA in MH-susceptible patients

The anesthesia machine (workstation) is a complex medical device that delivers anesthetic gases and oxygen to patients. Understanding its components is essential for safe practice.

🔧 Anesthesia Machine Components

Pre-Use Machine Check (Abbreviated)

1.
Check pipeline and cylinder pressures — O₂ pipeline 400 kPa, N₂O pipeline 400 kPa
2.
Check O₂ fail-safe — disconnect O₂ → N₂O should cut off; hypoxic guard functioning
3.
Check flowmeters — O₂, N₂O, air flow smoothly and accurately
4.
Check vaporiser — filled, correctly mounted, no tipping, interlock working
5.
Check breathing circuit — no leaks, correct connections, fresh gas flow outlet
6.
Check soda lime — colour appropriate, no harness (granule hardening)
7.
Check ventilator — test with test lung; PEEP, rate, tidal volume settings
8.
Check monitors — ECG, SpO₂, NIBP, EtCO₂, vapour analyser, temperature probe
9.
Check suction — powerful, working, Yankauer catheter connected
10.
Check airway equipment — ETT, LMA, videolaryngoscope, bougie, difficult airway kit accessible
